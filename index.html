About Us

Marmoris is an early-stage start-up that uses Remote Sensing, Geographical information Systems, and Artificial Intelligence (RS-GIS-AI) to support coastal ecosystem conservation and restoration efforts.

Our story began when we won first prize in a hackathon during which we developed space technology solutions for coral reef restoration. Since then, we have made it our mission to bring RS-GIS-AI in service of the practitioners on the ground, helping to strengthen their planning and management decisions with data-driven analytics and insights.

We are fully aware of the great potential that earth observation technologies combined with data science and software development can offer. But to reach this potential and provide actionable insights, they must be made easy to access, easy to interpret, and easy to integrate into the user’s own system.

It is our strong belief that data and technology are only as useful as the information they provide is meaningful to people working on the ground to solve the problem. Hence, we put continuous effort into engaging and communicating with stakeholders so we can adapt and improve our solutions as we develop.

Our initiative and efforts are strengthened by the support and guidance from two of the most important entrepreneurial programs in Europe: ESA business incubation, and Copernicus Accelerator.

Services

Monitoring, early warning tool

A simple web-based platform that brings together the most relevant near real time data and information for monitoring the status of your area of interest. You can set up indicators for which you would like to receive a warning if they occur (e.g. unusual temperatures for the season, algal bloom developing). This will help you take early action and implement protective measures.

Decision support system

In a decision support system, we combine data from different sources and add time series analysis to understand the historical behaviour of different phenomena in your area of interest. We then apply prediction models and geospatial analysis to perform risk assessment and answer questions related to the identification of threatened areas or the selection of optimal sites for coral conservation.

Geospatial analysis

Tools and systems are great assets for continuous monitoring and support to improve workflows. But other users may only require statistics, maps, graphs or a comprehensive case study report resulting from geospatial analysis, for one point in time. Through close collaboration and engagement with the user we define the key information that is needed to complement their existing data and knowledge and we provide insights in the optimal format.
